About Me

Here's a brief summary about who I am.

Hi! My name is Ivy and I just recently graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Information Systems from Cal State LA. I intend to go into business/data analytics, so the skills that I've learned both in school and in my own time leans towards that field. A few examples of the skills I've learned are SQL, Python, Excel, Tableau, SAS, SAP, Power BI, SharePoint, and OBIEE. I am currently still working to improve my proficiency in these skills though.

WHAT I'M CURRENTLY WORKING ON: Right now, I am interning at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. I started in April 2022 when I was still in school, so I was going to school full time and working as an intern part time. After I graduated in May 2022, I started working as an intern full time. So far, I've gain experience in maintaining and updating internal sites, as well as creating financial dashboards. In my free time, I continue researching ways that'd make my work processes easier or learning more about the tools I use at work but don't know so much about. Other than that, I do spend my weekends with family & friends, or getting my errands done.

HOW I DECIDED ON MY MAJOR: Prior to becoming a Computer Information Systems major, I was in Computer Science. I took various mathematics courses, such as Calculus I, Calculus II, and Linear Algebra, including various Computer Science courses where I learned how to program in Java and also developed my interest in SQL. It was not until the last half of my 2nd year when I decided to switch to CIS. I realized although programming was something important to learn and I should learn, it was something that I didn't want to entirely focus on. Something I also noticed was that I was always interested in learning more about how businesses develop, ran, and grew, which eventually led to my decision to switch to CIS so I could take business courses.

HOW I LEARNED: At my school, there were not a lot of courses that pertains to business or data analytics, but I was able to took all that was offered. Some skills were learned on my own, through YouTube, reading online articles, watching tutorials on LinkedIn Learning, etc. Although I can say I know the basics of all the skills I mentioned earlier, I know I need to become more proficient in those skills, which is why I continue working on projects, self-learning, and looking for a way to gain work experience.
